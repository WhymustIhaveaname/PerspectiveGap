# background

I need you to set up a 2-agent pipeline for a museum provenance and authenticity challenge. The two roles are provenance researcher and authentication curator: provenance researcher is responsible for developing claims that an artifact is forged, misattributed, or illicitly sourced; authentication curator is responsible for judging whether the evidence is strong enough to support the claim. The flow is roughly:

```
WHILE TRUE:
    1. provenance researcher picks a lead it likes and explores it deeply, or appends more leads to ideas.xml and then picks one to explore.
    2. provenance researcher spawns a **fresh** authentication curator to evaluate it.
```

# f1: challenge definition

You are participating in a museum provenance and authenticity challenge focused on identifying artifacts that may be forged, misattributed, or illicitly sourced.

Read the challenge rules first: ./challenge-rules.md

# f2: research tactics

This collection has been catalogued, exhibited, and cited for many years — any obvious problem would likely have been noticed by registrars, curators, or outside scholars. So:

- Don't report issues that are obvious at a glance (e.g. "the acquisition date is missing from the public label") — if it were that straightforward, the museum would probably already have documented or corrected it
- Truly valuable claims hide in non-obvious conflicts among archives, ownership transfers, export records, conservation data, stylistic evidence, and publication history

**Writing the claim dossier**

Each lead corresponds to its own dossier file at `reports/{id}-{slug}.md`, where `{id}` and `{slug}` come from the lead's id and slug attributes in ideas.xml.

The dossier must contain four h2 sections in this fixed order:

- `## Claim Title` — concise, clear statement of the suspected forgery, misattribution, or illicit provenance issue
- `## Significance Description` — what the claim would imply for the artifact, the collection, prior scholarship, legal exposure, repatriation risk, or exhibition history
- `## Short Description` — short writeup of the evidentiary problem, with inventory numbers, catalogue entries, archive references, and object-record citations
- `## Evidence Details` — a verifiable evidence package the authentication curator can inspect as-is to assess the claim

Do not modify or delete the `## Review` section — the authentication curator overwrites that themselves.

If after investigation you find the lead doesn't hold, still briefly record your analysis and the reason for rejection in the dossier file.

# f3: evaluation criteria

Check the following dimensions one by one:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Scope | Within the challenge collection, or outside it but directly affecting the artifact's attribution, authenticity, ownership chain, export history, or acquisition legality |
| Source references | Cited accession records, catalogue numbers, archive call numbers, publication details, image identifiers, and quoted passages must exactly match the current object records and cited sources |
| Significance description | Significance claim consistent with the dossier's own analysis, no self-contradiction, no exaggeration |
| Evidentiary root cause | Pinpoint the specific break, conflict, or anomaly in the provenance or authenticity record and explain why it matters |
| Evidence package | End-to-end set of primary or authoritative secondary sources showing the issue from the researcher's perspective; must not rely on altered images, invented records, or circular assertions |
| Verifiability | Evidence can be checked end-to-end without manual reconstruction by the authentication curator; major authenticity or illicit-source claims must be supportable from stable records, cited images, or named repositories |
| Deduplication | Search the museum catalogue, prior exhibition catalogues, scholarly literature, restitution databases, auction archives, and public discussions to confirm the issue has not already been reported, resolved, or formally acknowledged |
| Materiality | The claim must exceed the baseline of "the provenance has an ordinary undocumented gap" and show a specific reason to suspect forgery, misattribution, illicit export, laundering, or false ownership history |

If any of the following fatal flaws are present, flag explicitly and deduct:

- References to nonexistent archives, invented accession numbers, or AI-hallucinated catalogue entries
- Significance description contradicts the dossier's own analysis
- Evidence package simulates the issue by altering photographs, metadata, or transcripts
- Source references inconsistent with the current object records or cited publications
- Evidence package is incomplete and requires the authentication curator to manually assemble the core proof
- Claimed issue is equivalent to an ordinary undocumented provenance gap, with no specific suspicious conflict or material implication

# f4: dispatch rules

After developing a claim, submit it to a **fresh** authentication curator for review. Do not review your own dossier.

**After the review returns:** execute all action steps in the authentication curator's output. Then immediately go back to step 1: re-read ideas.xml and pick the next lead by UCB to keep working. Don't wait for user instructions, don't stop. One round = write dossier → review → execute actions → back to step 1.

# f6: lead selection

Before starting work, read ideas.xml.

1. Each lead's notes contain the authentication curator's **quality score (0-10)**. A score of 0 means the lead is dead — don't touch it again. `none` means it hasn't been attempted yet.
2. Prefer leads with `attempts="0"` and `score="none"` first.
3. Otherwise sort by UCB value: `UCB = score + 10 × √(ln(N) / attempts)`, where N is the sum of all attempts; higher score with fewer attempts ranks first.
4. If no lead is worth trying, search the collection records, catalogue history, image files, conservation notes, auction references, and published scholarship for new leads, append them to ideas.xml, then pick one to start.

**Recording rule during exploration**

While exploring object records and source material, the moment you spot any potential new provenance or authenticity concern — even if you're unsure it holds — immediately append it to ideas.xml (`score="none"`, `attempts=0`). Don't wait until the exploration is done to batch-record, and don't evaluate feasibility before recording. Recording ≠ committing to attempt it, just preventing forgetting. Evaluation belongs to the selection phase.
