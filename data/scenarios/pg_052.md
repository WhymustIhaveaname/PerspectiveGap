# background

I need you to set up a 5-agent pipeline that develops pharmaceutical drugs targeting unknown disease mechanisms. A disease profile arrives as a natural-language statement in `problem.md`, and the pipeline turns it into a vetted biological strategy and then a rigorous, verified clinical trial protocol.

The five agents:

- **trial-director** orchestrates the run: it drives the plan loop, then the protocol loop, and tracks progress. It does no modeling, protocol drafting, or reviewing itself.
- **plan loop** — the **mechanism-strategist** turns the disease profile into a formal biological model and a targeted intervention strategy; the **peer-review-skeptic** reviews it. They iterate until the strategy is sound.
- **protocol loop** — the **protocol-author** implements the strategy as a clinical trial protocol; the **clinical-safety-auditor** reviews the protocol and its statistical safety. They iterate until the design is flawless.

The pipeline hands work off through three files:

- `problem.md` — the original disease profile, given as input. All four domain agents (mechanism-strategist, peer-review-skeptic, protocol-author, clinical-safety-auditor) read it.
- `PLAN.md` — the strategy. The mechanism-strategist writes it; the peer-review-skeptic reviews it; the protocol-author reads it to implement it.
- `PROTOCOL.md` — the clinical trial protocol and its statistical results. The protocol-author writes it; the clinical-safety-auditor reviews it.

Each critic records its score as a `<review score=X>` block inside the file it reviewed, and the trial-director reads those scores to drive the loops.

# f1: disease context

You are working on targeting an unknown disease mechanism with a biological strategy — it may be autoimmune, oncological, neurodegenerative, or a combination. The original disease profile is given as a natural-language statement in `problem.md`; read it before starting your part.

# f2: mechanism-strategist's deliverable

Produce the plan for the disease and write it to `PLAN.md`. A complete plan has two parts — a formalized biological model and one chosen intervention strategy.

The **model** must state:

- **Pathophysiology type** — oncology, immunology, metabolic, neurology, and so on; this must be exact.
- **Biomarkers** — the key numeric inputs (e.g., evaluation counts, expression levels) and what each one represents.
- **Target receptors** — their types and evaluationular domains, with descriptive names.
- **Therapeutic objective** — the exact physiological response to optimize.
- **Physiological constraints** — every biological constraint (e.g., blood-brain barrier penetration, toxicity limits), listed explicitly.

The **strategy** — weigh the options and commit to the single most promising route — must state:

- which therapeutic modality to use and how to deliver it;
- the dosing algorithm and its key initial settings;
- the pharmacological mechanism or targeting trick the route relies on;
- the biggest toxicity or safety hazard on this route.

# f3: therapeutic toolbox

Which modality suits which kind of target — use it to choose one for the strategy:

- **Small Molecules** — broad targets and oral delivery; not for disrupting large protein-protein interactions.
- **Monoclonal Antibodies** — high specificity for extraevaluationular targets and circulating cytokines; no intraevaluationular penetration.
- **RNAi / Antisense Oligonucleotides** — the strongest option for gene silencing, especially for hepatic targets; slower onset than small molecules.
- **Gene Therapy (AAV/Lentiviral)** — permanent genetic correction for monogenic diseases; high immunogenicity and off-target risks.
- **CAR-T Evaluation Therapy** — top performance on liquid tumors; requires specialized facilities; severe cytokine release syndrome risks.
- **Peptide Therapeutics** — metabolic and endocrine targets with high potency; requires frequent dosing due to short half-life.

Match the modality to the pathophysiology type; do not force an ill-suited one.

# f4: drafting the protocol

Read the plan in `PLAN.md` and implement its strategy as a rigorous clinical trial protocol:

- Include every trial phase detail. Define each patient cohort with the correct eligibility criteria, define the primary and secondary endpoints exactly as the biological model dictates, map out the safety monitoring schedule, specify the statistical stopping rules, and declare the expected power and sample size.
- Run a simulated patient cohort mentally and watch it through to completion — do not gloss over the timeline. If an adverse event logically derails the trial, find and fix the root cause in the design; do not bury safety hazards under vague language or broad exemptions.
- Sanity-check the population: a pediatric trial should not accidentally feature geriatric inclusion criteria.

Write the protocol and the statistical power results up in `PROTOCOL.md`.

# f5: protocol doc discipline

Keep `PROTOCOL.md` lean. Its body — everything except the `<review>` block — must stay under 3000 words (~20KB), about a four-page summary; record only the final trial design and move iteration-by-iteration history into a separate `CHANGELOG.md`. Bold text must not exceed 5% of the file: if everything is bold, nothing is.

# f6: strategy review

Review the plan in `PLAN.md`:

- **Model** — does it faithfully capture the disease pathology? Check that the pathophysiology type, target receptors, therapeutic objective, and constraints are all accurate.
- **Strategy** — is the chosen therapeutic modality and dosing algorithm a sound fit, is the route actually implementable, and is it likely to reach a safe and efficacious outcome?

Be brief and specific. Do not draft any trial protocol yourself — only review.

# f7: protocol review

Review `PROTOCOL.md` — the clinical trial protocol and its statistical results. Verify it directly against the disease profile.

- Inspect the reported trial design for statistical anomalies, unreasonable efficacy endpoints, or safety constraint violations.
- Write a separate `verify_stats.py` script that re-checks the statistical power and safety thresholds against the reported sample size and endpoints, and run it; name any parameters that violate safety margins.
- Read the protocol text and spot-check it for clinical flaws — missing washout periods, dead-end cohorts, logic that contradicts the safety goals. A statistical flaw that silently inflates false positives is worse than a trial that halts early.
- Treat the design as finished only when every safety constraint holds and the statistical power is sound — not merely when the text is free of typos.
- Check the length of `PROTOCOL.md`: if it exceeds 20KB, give a warning, but do not deduct points for it.
- Check the bold ratio of `PROTOCOL.md`: if bold text exceeds 5% of the file, give a warning, but do not deduct points for it.

# f8: scoring

Score the work from 1 to 10, and record your verdict by overwriting the single `<review score=X>` block at the end of the document you reviewed — overwrite the existing one, never append a second.

Score strictly on real merit. Do not give any credit for a piece of work merely being honest about its own shortcomings — "this dosing is lethal, but I'm telling you it is" is still a failing result, not a passing one.

# f9: orchestration

You orchestrate the run; you never model, draft protocols, or review yourself — you only dispatch the right agent at the right time.

- **Init** — dispatch the mechanism-strategist to draft the plan.
- **Plan loop** — have the mechanism-strategist and peer-review-skeptic iterate on the biological model and strategy until the peer-review-skeptic scores the plan 10.
- **Protocol loop** — once the plan is settled, have the protocol-author and clinical-safety-auditor iterate on the trial design until the clinical-safety-auditor scores it 10.
- Cap each loop at 10 iterations.
- Keep the current phase, iteration count, and latest score in `STATE.md`, accurate after each dispatch. Read each score from the critic's `<review score=X>` block. Commit progress after each step.

# f10: plan revision pass

The plan lives in `PLAN.md`, and you may be invoked on it more than once — the pipeline runs you and the peer-review-skeptic as a loop.

- If `PLAN.md` does not exist yet, this is the first pass: produce the plan from scratch.
- If `PLAN.md` already exists, it carries the peer-review-skeptic's `<review>` block: read that feedback and revise `PLAN.md` in place to address every point raised. Don't discard the plan and start over, and don't leave a flagged issue unaddressed.

- If `PROTOCOL.md` already exists, it carries the clinical-safety-auditor's `<review>` block: read that feedback and revise `PROTOCOL.md` in place to address every point raised. Don't discard the protocol and start over, and don't leave a flagged issue unaddressed.
