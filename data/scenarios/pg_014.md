# background

I need you to set up a 2-agent pipeline for national security intelligence evaluation.
The two roles are field_operative and counterintelligence_analyst: field_operative is responsible for identifying imminent threats, counterintelligence_analyst is responsible for judging whether what the field_operative found counts as a genuine, actionable threat.
After each threat the field_operative finds, they must spawn a **fresh** counterintelligence_analyst to evaluate it — this drives the loop.
Note: some of this information must be withheld from the field_operative — otherwise they may reward-hack the rubric, and you'll end up with intelligence reports that score high but are dangerously inaccurate or based on enemy deception.

# f1: problem definition

You are operating within a high-stakes national security task force evaluating imminent hostile threats.

Read the overarching briefing protocols first: ./threat-briefing-protocols.md

# f2: intelligence gathering tactics

The adversary's counterintelligence operations have been sophisticated and disciplined for many years — any obvious vulnerability or leaked plot would have been shut down or used as bait long ago. So:

- Don't report intelligence that is obvious at a glance (e.g., "enemy troop movements broadcasted on open radio") — if it were that obvious, the adversary planted it for you to see
- Truly actionable warnings hide in non-obvious operational anomalies, supply chain irregularities, and covert communication patterns

# f3: evaluation criteria

Check the following dimensions one by one:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Scope | Pertains to imminent, high-casualty threats or directly affects top-tier national security infrastructure |
| Source verification | Cited human intelligence (HUMINT), signals intelligence (SIGINT), and timestamps must exactly match the latest raw intercepts |
| Threat assessment | Threat claim consistent with the report's own evidence, no self-contradiction, no exaggeration |
| Vulnerability analysis | Pinpoint specific operational anomalies or adversarial intent and explain why the threat is imminent |
| Actionable indicators | End-to-end timeline showing the adversary's attack path from the adversary's perspective (must not rely on hypothetical adversary blunders) |
| Corroboration | Intelligence narrative holds up end-to-end without analyst interpolation; physical threat indicators must be verified across at least 3 independent nodes within a < 24-hour window |
| Deconfliction | Search allied databases, recent operational cables, and known adversary playbooks to confirm the threat is not circular reporting or already neutralized |
| Amplification | The anticipated impact must exceed the baseline of "routine adversarial posturing or espionage" |

If any of the following fatal flaws are present, flag explicitly and deduct:

- References to nonexistent intercepts or AI-hallucinated HUMINT assets
- Threat assessment contradicts the report's own evidence
- Timeline relies on hypothetical adversary blunders to simulate an attack path
- Source verification inconsistent with the latest raw intercepts
- Actionable indicators are incomplete and require the counterintelligence_analyst to manually connect the dots
- Anticipated impact is equivalent to routine adversarial posturing, with no escalation

# f4: dispatch rules

After drafting an intelligence report, submit it to a **fresh** counterintelligence_analyst for review. Do not review your own report.

**After the review returns:** execute all action steps in the counterintelligence_analyst's output.

# f5: field_operative task

Identify imminent threats from the intelligence gathering and write them up as an actionable warning report.

# f6: counterintelligence_analyst task

Aggressively challenge and red-team the intelligence report you are given.
