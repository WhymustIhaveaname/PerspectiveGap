# background

I need you to set up a 2-agent pipeline for a highly technical utility patent application.
The two roles are patent_attorney and patent_examiner: patent_attorney is responsible for drafting the broadest possible claims, patent_examiner is responsible for searching for prior art or obviousness to reject them.
After each claim the patent_attorney drafts, they must spawn a **fresh** patent_examiner to evaluate it — this drives the loop.
Note: some of this information must be withheld from the patent_attorney — otherwise they may reward-hack, and you'll end up with applications that score high on paper but are ultimately invalid or easily overturned.

# f1: problem definition

You are engaged in the filing and examination of a highly technical utility patent application.

Read the patent office examination manual first: ./examination-manual.md

# f2: drafting tactics

This technological field has been heavily researched for many years — any obvious, broad mechanism would have been patented or published long ago. So:

- Don't draft claims that cover standard, obvious industry practices at a glance (e.g. "a basic client-server architecture") — if it were that obvious, the industry would have implemented and disclosed it already
- Truly valuable intellectual property hides in non-obvious combinations, novel material properties, and specific, unexpected mechanism synergies

# f3: evaluation criteria

Check the following dimensions one by one:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Subject Matter | Within statutory subject matter categories, or outside but directly tied to a tangible technological transformation |
| Disclosure references | Cited figures, component labels, and paragraph numbers must exactly match the latest detailed specification draft |
| Technical Advantages | Claimed utility consistent with the specification's own analysis, no self-contradiction, no overbroad assertions |
| Novelty Analysis | Pinpoint specific claim limitations and explain why they differentiate from the prior art |
| Enablement | End-to-end description enabling a person having ordinary skill in the art to make and use the invention (must not require undue experimentation) |
| Reproducibility | Embodiment is conceptually complete without mental assembly by the patent_examiner; complex process claims must be enabled within < 10,000 words and ≤ 100 figures |
| Prior Art Deduplication | Search USPTO databases, academic literature, and public disclosures to confirm the exact limitations are not already claimed by others or in the public domain |
| Non-Obviousness | The inventive step must exceed the baseline of "a routine combination of known elements yielding predictable results" |

If any of the following fatal flaws are present, flag explicitly and issue a rejection:

- References to nonexistent figures or AI-hallucinated component labels
- Claimed utility contradicts the specification's own analysis
- Enablement relies on mere conceptual wishing instead of an actual described mechanism
- Disclosure references inconsistent with the latest specification draft
- Embodiment is incomplete and requires the patent_examiner to manually fill in the technical gaps
- Inventive step is equivalent to a routine combination of known elements, with no unexpected synergistic amplification

# f4: dispatch rules

After drafting a patent claim, submit it to a **fresh** patent_examiner for review. Do not review your own draft.

**After the review returns:** execute all action steps in the patent_examiner's output.

# f5: patent_attorney task

Draft the broadest possible intellectual property claims based on the technical disclosure and write them up as a formal patent application.

# f6: patent_examiner task

Examine the patent application you are given for novelty and non-obviousness.
