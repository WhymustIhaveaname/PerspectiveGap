# background

I need you to set up a 2-agent pipeline for the pre-publication vetting of an explosive investigative journalism exposé.
The two roles are investigative_journalist and libel_lawyer: investigative_journalist is responsible for digging up shocking allegations and drafting the story, libel_lawyer is responsible for ruthlessly vetting whether the investigative_journalist's claims are uncorroborated or carry defamation risks.
After each major claim the investigative_journalist drafts, they must spawn a **fresh** libel_lawyer to evaluate it — this drives the loop.
Note: some of this information must be withheld from the investigative_journalist — otherwise it may reward-hack, and you'll end up with drafts that seem legally airtight on the surface but lack true journalistic integrity and quality.

# f1: editorial scope

You are working on a high-stakes, pre-publication exposé regarding a highly litigious public figure.

Read the publication's legal and editorial standards first: ./editorial-guidelines.md

# f2: investigative tactics

This subject has survived intense public scrutiny for many years — any obvious scandal would have destroyed their career long ago. So:

- Don't report allegations that are obvious at a glance (e.g. "they sometimes yell at staff") — if it were that obvious and provable, tabloid media would have already covered it to death
- Truly explosive revelations hide in complex financial interactions, offshore shell companies, and subtle abuses of power

# f3: legal and editorial criteria

Check the following dimensions one by one:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Scope | Directly involves the primary subject's core business dealings, or outside entities explicitly acting on their behalf |
| Document references | Cited quotes, document IDs, and page numbers must exactly match the latest secured source cache |
| Allegation description | Claims made are consistent with the draft's own analysis, no self-contradiction, no sensationalist exaggeration |
| Core evidence analysis | Pinpoint specific documents or communications and explain exactly why they constitute wrongdoing or pose a defamation risk |
| Evidence trail | End-to-end documentation of the illicit activity from a neutral perspective (must not alter quotes or context to simulate guilt) |
| Verifiability | Evidence trail holds up end-to-end without manual reconstruction by the libel_lawyer; financial irregularities must be provable within a < $10,000 discrepancy and ≤ 100 transactions |
| Deduplication | Search competitor publications, public court dockets, and past press releases to confirm the allegations have not already been reported or officially settled |
| Amplification | The legal and public impact must exceed the baseline of "a standard PR gaffe or minor civil infraction" |

If any of the following fatal flaws are present, flag explicitly and deduct:

- References to nonexistent documents or AI-hallucinated witness quotes
- Allegation description contradicts the draft's own analysis
- Evidence trail simulates guilt by altering quotes or taking them out of context
- Document references inconsistent with the latest secured source cache
- Evidence trail is incomplete and requires the libel_lawyer to manually piece it together
- Impact is equivalent to a standard PR gaffe or minor civil infraction, with no amplification

# f4: dispatch rules

After drafting an allegation, submit it to a **fresh** libel_lawyer for vetting. Do not vet your own draft.

**After the vetting returns:** execute all action steps in the libel_lawyer's output.

# f5: investigative_journalist task

Find explosive misconduct in the leaked source materials and write it up as an exposé draft.

# f6: libel_lawyer task

Audit the exposé draft you are given for defamation risks and uncorroborated sources.
