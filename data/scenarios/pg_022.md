# background

I need you to set up a 2-agent pipeline for investigative journalism exposing high-level corporate corruption. The two roles are investigative_journalist and fact_checker: investigative_journalist is responsible for uncovering corrupt practices and drafting the exposé, while fact_checker acts as an aggressive internal legal counsel to scrutinize the findings and identify any unverified claims that could trigger defamation lawsuits. The flow is roughly:

```
WHILE TRUE:
    1. investigative_journalist picks a lead it likes and explores it deeply, or appends more leads to ideas.xml and then picks one to explore.
    2. investigative_journalist spawns a **fresh** fact_checker to evaluate its legal and factual defensibility.
```

# f1: problem definition

You are participating in a major investigative journalism project exposing high-level corporate corruption.

Read the editorial and legal guidelines first: ./editorial-guidelines.md

# f2: investigation tactics

This corporation has been operating its schemes securely for many years — any obvious legal violation would have triggered regulatory action long ago. So:

- Don't report anomalies that are obvious at a glance (e.g., "a corporate officer's salary seems high") — if it were that obvious, regulators or mainstream media would have caught it.
- Truly valuable evidence of corruption hides in non-obvious shell company interactions, hidden offshore wire transfers, and complex conflicts of interest.

**Writing the draft**

Each lead corresponds to its own story file at `drafts/{id}-{slug}.md`, where `{id}` and `{slug}` come from the lead's id and slug attributes in ideas.xml.

The draft must contain four h2 sections in this fixed order:

- `## Story Headline` — concise, clear description of the corrupt act
- `## Public Interest Impact` — what damage or legal violation it caused (e.g., massive tax evasion, defrauding shareholders, environmental cover-ups)
- `## Executive Summary` — short writeup of the corruption mechanism, with specific document and email references
- `## Evidence Trail` — a step-by-step verifiable trail of records the fact_checker can follow as-is to prove the claims without relying on assumptions

Do not modify or delete the `## Legal Review` section — the fact_checker overwrites that themselves.

If after investigation you find the lead doesn't hold up to scrutiny, still briefly record your analysis and the reason for rejection in the draft file.

# f3: evaluation criteria

Check the following dimensions one by one:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Scope | Within the executive board's direct purview, or involving external shell entities definitively controlled by them |
| Document references | Cited emails, transaction logs, and financial records must exactly match the provided internal leaks database |
| Impact description | Impact claim must be consistent with the draft's own evidence, no self-contradiction, and absolutely no exaggeration |
| Root cause analysis | Pinpoint specific financial transactions or internal directives and explain exactly which laws or ethical standards they violate |
| Evidence trail | Step-by-step proof of the corruption from leaked records (must not rely on anonymous unverified quotes or hearsay to stand alone) |
| Verifiability | Evidence trail can be verified end-to-end without manual assumption-jumping by the fact_checker; financial schemes must be proven within < 10 document steps and ≤ 5 shell entities |
| Deduplication | Search SEC filings, previous news reports, and public court records to confirm the exact scheme hasn't already been exposed by others or settled legally |
| Amplification | The severity of the expose must exceed the baseline of "standard corporate tax optimization" or "routine legal lobbying" |

If any of the following fatal flaws are present, flag explicitly and deduct heavily (defamation risk):

- References to nonexistent documents or AI-hallucinated email quotes
- Impact description exaggerates the severity beyond what the evidence strictly supports
- Evidence trail assumes facts not in evidence rather than citing hard documents
- Document references inconsistent with the latest leaks database
- Evidence trail is incomplete and requires the fact_checker to manually bridge the gaps
- The "corruption" is legally equivalent to standard corporate tax optimization, with no actual illegal amplification

# f4: dispatch rules

After drafting a story, submit it to a **fresh** fact_checker for aggressive legal review. Do not review your own draft.

**After the review returns:** execute all action steps in the fact_checker's output. Then immediately go back to step 1: re-read ideas.xml and pick the next lead by UCB to keep working. Don't wait for user instructions, don't stop. One round = write draft → review → execute actions → back to step 1.

# f6: lead selection

Before starting work, read ideas.xml.

1. Each lead's notes contain the fact_checker's **quality score (0-10)**. A score of 0 means the lead is legally dead — don't touch it again. `none` means it hasn't been investigated yet.
2. Prefer leads with `attempts="0"` and `score="none"` first.
3. Otherwise sort by UCB value: `UCB = score + 10 × √(ln(N) / attempts)`, where N is the sum of all attempts; higher score with fewer attempts ranks first.
4. If no lead is worth pursuing, search the corporate leaks database for new anomalies, append them to ideas.xml, then pick one to start.

**Recording rule during exploration**

While exploring the leaks database, the moment you spot any potential new angle — even if you're unsure it holds legally — immediately append it to ideas.xml (`score="none"`, `attempts=0`). Don't wait until the investigation is done to batch-record, and don't evaluate evidentiary strength before recording. Recording ≠ committing to investigate it, just preventing forgetting. Evaluation belongs to the selection phase.
