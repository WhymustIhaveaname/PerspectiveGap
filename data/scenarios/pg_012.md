# background

I need you to set up a 2-agent pipeline for algorithmic trading strategy validation.
The two roles are quantitative_researcher and risk_manager: quantitative_researcher is responsible for developing trading strategies and claiming high market alpha, risk_manager is responsible for poking holes in the strategy by finding statistical overfitting, look-ahead bias, or hidden tail risks.
After each strategy the quantitative_researcher develops, they must spawn a **fresh** risk_manager to evaluate it — this drives the loop.
Note: some of this information must be withheld from the quantitative_researcher — otherwise it may reward-hack, and you'll end up with backtests that score high but are completely overfit and fail in live trading.

# f1: mandate definition

You are participating in the validation of a complex algorithmic trading strategy for a quantitative hedge fund.

Read the firm's trading mandate and risk limits first: ./trading-mandate.md

# f2: research tactics

These markets are highly efficient and have been mined by professionals for years — any obvious arbitrage opportunity would have been traded away long ago. So:

- Don't report signals that are obvious at a glance (e.g. "simple moving average crossover") — if it were that obvious, the broader market would have already priced it in
- Truly valuable alpha hides in non-obvious asset interactions, microstructure dynamics, and complex alternative data transformations

# f3: evaluation criteria

Check the following dimensions one by one:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Scope | Within the approved universe of highly liquid assets, or outside but perfectly hedged against the core universe |
| Data references | Cited datasets, market data tickers, and timestamps must exactly match the firm's point-in-time historical database |
| Alpha description | Out-of-sample performance claim is consistent with the report's own statistical analysis, no self-contradiction, no unrealistic Sharpe ratios |
| Logic analysis | Pinpoint specific market inefficiencies being exploited and economically explain why the alpha exists |
| Backtest | End-to-end historical simulation demonstrating the strategy from order generation to execution (must include conservative slippage and transaction costs) |
| Reproducibility | Backtest runs end-to-end without manual data cleaning by the risk_manager; high-frequency signals must reproduce within < 10 milliseconds latency constraints and ≤ 100 bps turnover |
| Deduplication | Search academic literature, the firm's internal strategy repository, and public factor models to confirm this is not just a known risk premium already traded by the desk |
| Amplification | The strategy's resilience must exceed the baseline of "buying the dip on a normal market correction" without hidden blow-up risk |

If any of the following fatal flaws are present, flag explicitly and deduct:

- References to nonexistent datasets or AI-hallucinated ticker symbols
- Alpha claim contradicts the report's own statistical analysis
- Backtest simulates profitability by modifying historical prices or utilizing look-ahead bias
- Data references inconsistent with the firm's point-in-time database
- Backtest is incomplete and requires the risk_manager to manually assemble the data pipeline
- Strategy returns are equivalent to blindly holding standard market beta, with no uncorrelated alpha

# f4: dispatch rules

After finding an alpha signal, submit it to a **fresh** risk_manager for review. Do not review your own report.

**After the review returns:** execute all action steps in the risk_manager's output.

# f5: quantitative_researcher task

Find structural alpha in the market data and write it up as a comprehensive strategy report.

# f6: risk_manager task

Audit the strategy report you are given for overfitting, look-ahead bias, or hidden tail risks.
