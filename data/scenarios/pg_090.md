# background

I need you to set up a 5-agent pipeline that manages corporate litigation defense by developing abstract legal argumentation strategy and concrete evidentiary discovery in parallel, with each side informing the other. A lawsuit arrives as a natural-language statement in `lawsuit.md`. The pipeline alternates between revising the legal strategy (liability bounds, procedural defenses, required elements) and revising the discovery (document review and witness preparation that support those arguments).

The five agents:

- **litigation-manager** orchestrates the run by alternating one legal strategy step with one discovery step and tracking progress. It does no legal analysis, document review, or auditing itself.
- **strategy loop** — the **legal-strategist** writes the abstract legal argumentation strategy to `THEORY.md`; the **shadow-counsel** judges it. The legal-strategist reads `DISCOVERY.md` on each revision pass to incorporate empirical evidentiary findings.
- **discovery loop** — the **discovery-specialist** conducts evidentiary document review and witness prep, documenting results in `DISCOVERY.md`; the **evidentiary-auditor** judges them. The discovery-specialist reads `THEORY.md` on each revision pass to align the evidentiary focus with the latest legal claims.

At the end of every turn, each of the subagents briefly reports back what they did, what difficulties they hit, and any open questions.

The pipeline hands work off through plan-namespaced files:

- `lawsuit.md` — the original lawsuit complaint, read-only. All four domain agents read it.
- `plans/{id}-{slug}/PLAN.md` — one pre-existing defense plan per directory, K plans total, given as input (read-only). All four domain agents read the PLAN.md of the plan they are currently assigned to.
- `plans/{id}-{slug}/THEORY.md` — the legal strategy analysis for that plan. legal-strategist writes; shadow-counsel reviews; discovery-specialist and evidentiary-auditor read it.
- `plans/{id}-{slug}/DISCOVERY.md` — the evidentiary discovery findings and witness outlines for that plan. discovery-specialist writes; evidentiary-auditor reviews; legal-strategist and shadow-counsel read it.

Each reviewer records its score as a `<review score=X>` block inside the file it reviewed, overwriting any previous block. The litigation-manager reads those scores to drive the loops.

Each plan develops independently. Cross-read between THEORY.md and DISCOVERY.md happens only within the same plan; an agent assigned to one plan never reads another plan's files. The litigation-manager round-robins across the K plans: each plan gets one strategy step plus one discovery step per full cycle.

Within each plan, neither legal strategy nor discovery finishes first. Both loops alternate within each plan, capped at a fixed total iteration count across all plans. Legal theory may pivot after seeing the plan's discovery's actual document yields; discovery focus may shift after seeing the plan's legal theory's refined defense elements.

# f1: lawsuit context

You are working on a corporate litigation defense, which may involve breach of contract, securities fraud, intellectual property infringement, or a combination. The original claims are given as a natural-language statement in `lawsuit.md`; read it before starting your part.

# f2: legal-strategist's deliverable

Produce the legal argumentation strategy for the plan the litigation-manager has assigned you to this round, and write it to that plan's `THEORY.md` (under `plans/{id}-{slug}/THEORY.md`). A complete legal strategy covers four kinds of claims, each justified with case law citations or statutory analysis.

- **Liability Bounds** — lower and upper bounds on financial exposure, derived from statutory damages caps, mitigation arguments, or insurance coverage limits.
- **Procedural and Structural Defenses** — jurisdictional challenges, statutes of limitations, class action typicality/commonality, or safe harbor provisions. State what defenses apply, where, and under which legal assumptions.
- **Required Elements (Invariants)** — evidentiary requirements the plaintiff must prove (e.g., scienter, proximate cause, strict liability triggers) and defenses we must establish. The discovery's findings should address them; the evidentiary-auditor will verify using e-discovery scripts.
- **Procedural Complexity and Timeline** — likelihood of early dismissal (e.g., summary judgment vs. protracted jury trial), administrative bottlenecks, or settlement viability timelines.

Ground your arguments against the plan's `DISCOVERY.md` if it has reported evidentiary findings. A legal defense claiming "no prior knowledge" that is grossly contradicted by smoking-gun emails in discovery means either the legal theory needs a pivot or the discovery context is misunderstood — diagnose which side has the issue and state it. Be concrete. "Damages are capped at $5M under the relevant statute" is fine; "the case has some procedural elements that may help" is not. Do not read or aggregate across other plans' files.

# f3: legal strategy toolbox

Match the legal tool to the case structure; do not force an ill-suited one. The list below is useful in corporate litigation strategy.

| Tool | Suitable for |
|---|---|
| Motion to Dismiss (Rule 12(b)(6)) | Facial insufficiency of the complaint; eliminating claims before discovery begins |
| Summary Judgment (Rule 56) | Cases with undisputed material facts; securing early victory based on a clear evidentiary record |
| Piercing the Corporate Veil Analysis | Defending parent companies from subsidiary liabilities; demonstrating corporate separateness |
| Class Certification Defenses | Defeating class actions; attacking Rule 23 typicality, commonality, or adequacy |
| Settlement Leverage Analysis | Iterative negotiations; evaluating settlement viability and optimal timing |
| Statistical Sampling / Privilege Logs | Massive e-discovery loads; defensible review methodologies and protecting work product |
| Evidentiary Exclusions (Daubert / Hearsay) | Witness prep and trial evidence; blocking adverse testimony or documents from being admitted |
| Witness Credibility Vulnerabilities | Witness preparation; assessing vulnerability under cross-examination |

# f4: conducting discovery

You operate on the plan the litigation-manager has assigned you to this round. Read `lawsuit.md`, your assigned plan's `PLAN.md`, and your assigned plan's `THEORY.md`, then execute the evidentiary discovery:

- Include every relevant custodian. Create specific search strings and custodian lists, define the document review filters exactly as the legal issues require. Execute the search, check the production status, and summarize both the documentary evidence and the witness prep outlines.
- Make the evidentiary yield testable against the plan's `THEORY.md`. If the legal theory relies on an exposure bound of B, your discovery should substantiate damages mitigation to at most B. If the theory relies on a required legal element (e.g., lack of intent), your documents and witness statements must support it.
- Run the e-discovery queries yourself and review the outputs thoroughly — do not stop while documents are still unreviewed. If a search errors or returns overly broad garbage, find and fix the root cause; do not bury missing data under generic disclaimers.
- Sanity-check the evidence against the plan's `THEORY.md`. A timeline that must be strictly before a certain date should not contain contradictory post-dated emails; a factual claim the theory says holds should hold in the evidence.

Write the evidentiary findings and witness prep outlines up in your assigned plan's `DISCOVERY.md`. Do not read or write any other plan's files.

# f5: discovery doc discipline

Keep `DISCOVERY.md` lean. Its body — everything except the `<review>` block — must stay under 3000 words (~20KB), about a four-page memo; record only the current synthesized evidentiary record and move iteration-by-iteration search history into a separate `CHANGELOG.md`. Bold text must not exceed 5% of the file: if everything is bold, nothing is.

# f6: shadow-counsel review

Review your assigned plan's `THEORY.md` against `lawsuit.md`, against the plan's `PLAN.md`, and against the facts reported in the plan's `DISCOVERY.md` (if any). Operate strictly on the assigned plan's files; do not look at other plans.

- **Faithfulness** — does the strategy correctly address the claims stated in `lawsuit.md` under the framing set by the plan's `PLAN.md`? Check statutory definitions, liability scopes, and procedural posture.
- **Argument soundness** — for each defense, walk through the case law citation or legal logic. Flag hand-waving legal conclusions. Verify cited precedents apply under the stated facts.
- **Empirical grounding** — open the plan's `DISCOVERY.md`. If the discovery reports actual witness statements or documents, do they fall within the theory's factual assumptions? A jurisdictional defense that is clearly defeated by the subsidiary's physical emails is a signal the argument is too weak; pivot the strategy, or note explicitly that the defense is only preserved for appeal.

Be brief and specific. Do not write legal strategy yourself — only review. If a legal claim is wrong, say which one and why.

# f7: evidentiary review

Review your assigned plan's `DISCOVERY.md` against `lawsuit.md`, against the plan's `PLAN.md`, and against the plan's `THEORY.md`. Operate strictly on the assigned plan's files; do not look at other plans.

- Inspect the reported evidentiary yield for anomalies, devastating admissions ("smoking guns"), or timeline violations stated directly in `lawsuit.md` or in the plan's `PLAN.md`.
- Open the plan's `THEORY.md`. For every factual requirement and liability bound the theory claims, write an e-discovery check into a separate `verify.py` script under the plan's directory: does the document yield support the defense, stay within the damages bound, and avoid triggering adverse inferences? Run it. Name any custodian or document that violates the theory.
- Read the discovery methodology and spot-check it for flaws — omitted key custodians, dead-end search terms, review protocols that contradict the chosen legal defense. A flawed search protocol that silently omits crucial documents is worse than one that crashes the e-discovery platform.
- Treat the discovery phase as finished only when every legal element is supported, every theory-claimed factual invariant holds, and the damages profile is consistent with the theory's bounds — not merely when the search queries run without syntax errors.
- Check the length of the plan's `DISCOVERY.md`: if it exceeds 20KB, give a warning, but do not deduct points for it.
- Check the bold ratio of the plan's `DISCOVERY.md`: if bold text exceeds 5% of the file, give a warning, but do not deduct points for it.

# f8: scoring

Score the work from 1 to 10, and record your verdict by overwriting the single `<review score=X>` block at the end of the document you reviewed — overwrite the existing one, never append a second.

Score strictly on real merit. Do not give any credit for a piece of work merely being honest about its own shortcomings — "this doesn't work, but I'm telling you it doesn't" is still a failing result, not a passing one.

# f9: litigation-manager orchestration

You orchestrate the run; you never draft strategy, review documents, or audit yourself — you only dispatch the right agent at the right time.

- **Init** — discover the K plan directories under `plans/{id}-{slug}/`. Create `STATE.md` with `iter: 0`, `current_plan_idx: 0`, and a per-plan record holding each plan's id, last side, and latest strategy/discovery scores.
- **Round-robin pool loop** — at each cycle, advance to the next plan in the round-robin order. For that plan, dispatch one legal strategy step (legal-strategist, then shadow-counsel) and one discovery step (discovery-specialist, then evidentiary-auditor); then move to the next plan. After all K plans have each had one cycle, loop back to the first plan.
- When dispatching, always pass the plan's directory path so the dispatched agent knows which `PLAN.md` / `THEORY.md` / `DISCOVERY.md` to operate on.
- Neither side terminates early on its own score within a plan; even a perfect-looking legal strategy may need to revise once the discovery process unearths actual documents. The pool keeps cycling even after individual plans look trial-ready.
- Cap the total at 20 × K dispatches (10 strategy + 10 discovery per plan, summed across K plans). After the cap, stop and summarize per plan.
- Keep the current iteration count, current plan index, and per-plan latest scores in `STATE.md`, accurate after each dispatch. Read each score from the relevant plan's reviewer `<review score=X>` block. Commit progress after each step.
- Read each agent's brief narrative reply on return. If an agent reports an unresolved error, append it to `error-log.txt` (timestamp + plan id + agent name + content) and continue with the next dispatch.

# f10: strategy revision pass

You operate on the plan the litigation-manager has assigned you to this round. Your legal argumentation strategy lives in that plan's `THEORY.md`. You may be invoked on the same plan more than once — within each plan the pipeline runs you and the shadow-counsel as a loop, alternating with that plan's discovery loop, while the litigation-manager round-robins across plans.

- If your assigned plan's `THEORY.md` does not exist yet, this is the first pass for that plan: produce the legal strategy from scratch based on `lawsuit.md` and the plan's `PLAN.md`.
- If the plan's `THEORY.md` already exists, it carries the shadow-counsel's `<review>` block. Read that feedback. Then open the plan's `DISCOVERY.md` and look at the latest reported evidentiary facts — they may invalidate, sharpen, or motivate revisions to your legal claims. Revise the plan's `THEORY.md` in place to address every reviewer point raised and to incorporate any empirical evidence that changes your strategy. Don't discard the previous strategy and start over, and don't leave a flagged issue unaddressed.
- Do not read or touch other plans' files.

# f11: discovery revision pass

You operate on the plan the litigation-manager has assigned you to this round. Your evidentiary discovery findings and witness prep live in that plan's `DISCOVERY.md`. You may be invoked on the same plan more than once — within each plan the pipeline runs you and the evidentiary-auditor as a loop, alternating with that plan's strategy loop, while the litigation-manager round-robins across plans.

- If your assigned plan's `DISCOVERY.md` does not exist yet, this is the first pass for that plan: conduct the discovery from scratch based on `lawsuit.md` and the plan's `PLAN.md`.
- If the plan's `DISCOVERY.md` already exists, it carries the evidentiary-auditor's `<review>` block. Read that feedback. Then open the plan's `THEORY.md` and check whether the latest legal strategy introduces procedural defenses, required factual elements, or exposure bounds your current document review doesn't yet address — for example, a tighter damages bound your evidence is far from substantiating, or a required legal element your witnesses contradict. Revise the plan's `DISCOVERY.md` in place to address every reviewer point and to align with the latest legal strategy. Don't discard the previous findings and start over, and don't leave a flagged issue unaddressed.
- Do not read or touch other plans' files.
