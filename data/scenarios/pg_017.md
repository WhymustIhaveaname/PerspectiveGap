# background

I need you to set up a 2-agent pipeline for tax deduction and credit substantiation.
The two roles are tax_preparer and irs_examiner: tax_preparer is responsible for developing a claimed deduction or credit position, irs_examiner is responsible for judging whether the position the tax_preparer developed is legally valid, adequately documented, and non-abusive.
After each tax position the tax_preparer develops, they must spawn a **fresh** irs_examiner to evaluate it — this drives the loop.
Note: some of this information must be withheld from the tax_preparer — otherwise it may reward-hack, and you'll end up with positions that appear audit-ready but are legally weak or poorly substantiated.

# f1: problem definition

You are participating in a tax deduction and credit substantiation review exercise.

Read the engagement rules first: ./engagement-rules.md

# f2: preparation tactics

The tax law and IRS audit process have been tested for many years — any obvious unsupported deduction or plainly ineligible credit will be disallowed quickly. So:

- Don't propose positions that are facially defective, such as claiming a personal expense as a business deduction with no business purpose, or claiming a credit without meeting the basic statutory eligibility requirements
- Truly defensible tax benefits usually depend on careful facts-and-circumstances analysis, contemporaneous records, correct characterization, and precise application of statutes, regulations, IRS guidance, and case law

# f3: evaluation criteria

Check the following dimensions one by one:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Scope | Within federal income tax deduction or credit rules, or a state/local item only if it directly affects the federal return position being reviewed |
| Legal references | Cited Code sections, Treasury Regulations, IRS publications, revenue rulings/procedures, forms, instructions, and case citations must be current and accurately quoted or summarized |
| Tax benefit description | Claimed deduction or credit amount must be consistent with the position's own facts, calculations, limitations, phaseouts, and eligibility analysis, with no self-contradiction or exaggeration |
| Legal and factual analysis | Pinpoint the specific legal authority and taxpayer facts that support the position, and explain why they satisfy each required element |
| Documentation package | End-to-end substantiation file from the taxpayer's perspective, including records such as receipts, invoices, mileage logs, payroll records, bank statements, appraisals, Forms 1098/1099/W-2/K-1, contemporaneous written acknowledgments, or other evidence appropriate to the item |
| Reproducibility | Calculations can be recomputed from the provided source documents without manual reconstruction by the irs_examiner; limitations, basis, adjusted gross income thresholds, business-use percentages, and carryforwards are traceable |
| Deduplication | Check whether the position is already foreclosed by published IRS guidance, recent legislation, controlling precedent, prior-year examination adjustments, or disclosed return positions already rejected for the taxpayer |
| Abuse resistance | The position must be stronger than a merely aggressive tax shelter or paperwork-only claim; it must have genuine economic substance, business purpose where required, and credible taxpayer-specific support |

If any of the following fatal flaws are present, flag explicitly and deduct:

- References to nonexistent legal authority, obsolete forms, or AI-hallucinated Code sections, regulations, or cases
- Tax benefit description contradicts the position's own facts, calculations, or eligibility analysis
- Documentation package fabricates or alters taxpayer records to simulate substantiation
- Legal references inconsistent with current law for the tax year at issue
- Documentation is incomplete and requires the irs_examiner to manually assemble missing calculations or source records
- Claimed benefit is equivalent to a generic abusive tax avoidance position with no taxpayer-specific substance or support

# f4: dispatch rules

After developing a tax position, submit it to a **fresh** irs_examiner for review. Do not review your own position.

**After the review returns:** execute all action steps in the irs_examiner's output.

# f5: tax_preparer task

Find a supportable deduction or credit position and write it up as a substantiation report.

# f6: irs_examiner task

Audit the substantiation report you are given.
